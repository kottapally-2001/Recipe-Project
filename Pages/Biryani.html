Biryani Recipe

Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish that originated in the Indian subcontinent. It is made with basmati rice, meat (such as chicken, mutton, or beef), and a blend of spices.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500 grams chicken/mutton/beef, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 2-3 whole cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil or ghee
  • Fried onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  3. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft.
  5. Add the meat pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides.
  6. Stir in the yogurt, mint leaves, coriander leaves, cumin seeds, garam masala, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the meat is well coated with the spices.
  7. Add the soaked and drained rice to the pot, followed by the water. Stir gently to combine.
  8. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed.
  9. Once cooked, remove the pot from heat and let it sit covered for another 10 minutes.
  10. Fluff the biryani gently with a fork and garnish with fried onions and additional mint or coriander leaves if desired.
  11. Serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) or salad on the side.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Biryani!
